What is WatSize?

WatSize is a project being developed by fourth year Mechatronics Engineering students at the University of Waterloo, Canada, to reduce the cost, consumer inconvenience, and environmental impact of improper fit for clothing purchased online.

WatSize uses a custom built full-body scanner, state-of-the-art machine learning models, and a mobile app to provide users with clothing size recommendations.

Reduce company losses

Companies have lost over $62.4 billion due to online returns of apparel and footwear[1]. WatSize will help customers pick the best fitting size for their body, reducing the frequency and cost of returns.

Reduce environmental impact

By reducing the frequency of returns, WatSize will help decrease the 5 billion pounds of landfill waste and 15 million tonnes of carbon emissions produced by US returns annually[2].

Decrease consumer frustration

40% of online clothing purchases are returned[3], 52% of which are due to improper fit[4]. Consumers will often purchase the same article in multiple sizes, and return the ones that don't fit[1]. By providing recommended sizes, WatSize will help to reduce clothing size uncertainty and consumer frustration.

Scan body to provide size recommendations

Users can visit a nearby body scanner to get their body dimensions, shown in the mobile app. The app provides a store for purchasing supported clothing. Each clothing item in the store comes with a recommended size for the user, determined by comparing its dimensions with the user's.

Scanner

The WatSize scanner leverages LIDAR and camera technology to build a 3D model of your body, personalizing your clothing recommendations.

Machine Learning

Through state-of-the-art machine learning techniques the scanner measurements can be transformed into precise body measurements, helping find the best fit for your body.

Mobile App

The WatSize mobile app for Android and iOS connects users to nearby scanners, and securely stores their measurements for easy viewing. Through our app users can view and purchase supported clothing, for which they are provided with recommended sizes.
